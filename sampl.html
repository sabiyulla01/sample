Babies

Boys

Wash your hands or use hand sanitizer before handling your baby. Wash your baby's face and neck with lukewarm water or a mild baby soap and a soft washcloth.

Feeding: Breast milk is the natural way to feed your baby and meets all their needs for about the first 6 months. After that, you can introduce healthy solid foods while continuing to breastfeed.

Sleep: Infants 4–12 months old need 12–16 hours of sleep per 24 hours, including naps.

Immunizations: Get your child immunized as recommended.

Safety: Keep your baby safe by childproofing your home, securing furniture, and covering electrical outlets.

Screen time: Limit screen time for children under 18 months to video chatting.

Girl

Breastfeeding

Breast milk contains everything a baby needs for brain development, digestion, and protection from illness.

Role model

A mother's health habits, body image, weight, and attitude can strongly influence her daughter's health.

Frequent feeding

For low birth weight babies, feeding them in smaller amounts more frequently can help them gain weight.

Healthy drinks

For toddlers, drinks can sometimes replace snacks or food. This can reduce the variety of foods eaten and affect growth.

Healthy eating

Children who are encouraged to eat healthily and be active are more likely to stay a healthy weight.

Sleep

Sleep is essential for brain development and physical growth.

Diapers

Change diapers frequently, use fragrance-free wipes, and apply diaper cream to prevent diaper rash.
